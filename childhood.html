Childhood

I grew up in a village that was still developing, and this environment played a major role in shaping my character and perspective on life. Basic facilities and access to learning resources were sometimes limited, which meant that many everyday tasks required extra effort and determination. Despite these challenges, my family and community provided strong support, teaching me the importance of hard work, discipline, and cooperation. Living in a growing village also allowed me to witness progress over time, which inspired me to value education as a powerful tool for personal and community development. These experiences motivated me to remain focused on my academic goals and strengthened my determination to pursue a career in software engineering, where I hope to create technological solutions that can help improve lives in developing communities.

Formative experiences

Memory #1

One memorable event from my childhood occurred when I had to take greater responsibility at home during a period when my family needed additional support. Balancing schoolwork with household responsibilities was not easy, and at times it felt overwhelming. However, that experience taught me resilience, time management, and the importance of staying committed even when circumstances are challenging. It also strengthened my sense of responsibility and helped me understand that discipline and perseverance are essential qualities for achieving long-term goals.

Memory #2

One important moment that influenced my values was the guidance I received from my mother, who has always been my mentor. During my childhood, she regularly encouraged me to take my education seriously and remain disciplined in everything I did. Through her advice and daily example, she taught me that success comes from patience, hard work, and strong personal values. Her support and mentorship continue to motivate me to stay focused on my goals and work toward building a better future.

What I learned early

Value:Discipline
From an early age, I learned discipline by committing myself to completing my schoolwork on time and helping with responsibilities at home. Following daily routines and staying focused on my studies taught me the importance of consistency and self-control in achieving goals.
Value:Empathy
Growing up in a close-knit community helped me understand the importance of caring for others and supporting those around me. This developed my empathy, allowing me to build respectful and understanding relationships with friends, classmates, and family members.
Value:Curiosity
My curiosity encouraged me to constantly ask questions, explore new ideas, and seek knowledge beyond what was taught in class. This mindset helped me develop a strong interest in learning, especially in technology and problem-solving, which later inspired my passion for software engineering.
